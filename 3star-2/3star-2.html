Instructions

This is an advanced exercise! You may not finish it during the practical session.

Create your own version of a basic card game. Crazy Eights is recommended as it is fairly simple but still requires you to think carefully about your choice of data structures. You can choose a different game but make sure it is for two players AND requires the player to make a strategic choice. Games like Snap, which are based on pure luck and speed, are not appropriate.

Your user should play against the computer and your AI should be reasonably strategic. Choose your data structures so that your AI can make a good choice! For example, in Crazy Eights, players must match the suit or value of the last card played, or they can play an 8 and choose a new suit. If the player can't go, they have to draw new cards from the deck until they can go. As the objective of the game is to be the first to play all your cards, strategic decisions will seek to maximise the player's ability to play without drawing a new card, or to cause their opponent to draw one or more new cards. To make a strategic decision, search and sorting by suit and value is important.

The assets folder includes png files of all cards in a standard deck.
